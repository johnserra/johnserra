## Ingredients

- **4** center-cut veal shanks (approx. 1.5 inches thick)
- **1** large onion, finely diced
- **1** carrot, finely diced
- **1** celery stalk, finely diced
- **2** cloves garlic, minced
- **2** sprigs fresh rosemary
- **1** bay leaf
- **1 cup** dry wine (Frascati is traditional; Montepulciano d'Abruzzo is what I usually have on hand)
- **2 cups** beef or veal stock
- **1 can (14 oz)** crushed tomatoes or passata
- All-purpose flour (for dredging)
- Extra virgin olive oil, salt, and black pepper
- Kitchen twine

---

## Method

1. **Prep:** Tie kitchen twine around each shank to keep the meat on the bone. Pat dry, season with salt/pepper, and dredge lightly in flour.
2. **Sear:** In a Dutch oven, brown shanks in olive oil over medium-high heat (5 mins per side). Remove and set aside.
3. **Soffritto:** Sauté onion, carrot, and celery until soft (8 mins). Stir in garlic and rosemary for 1 min.
4. **Deglaze:** Add wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to release the _fond_. Reduce wine by half.
5. **Braise:** Add tomatoes, stock, and bay leaf. Return shanks to the pot (should be 3/4 submerged).
6. **Slow Cook:** Cover and simmer on low OR bake at **160°C (325°F)** for **2 to 2.5 hours** until meat is fork-tender.

---

## Notes

This is a classic Roman dish, perfect for a slow-cooked Sunday meal. Serve with risotto or rustic mashed potatoes.
